Final prototype

September 5, 2021



Finally after more than a year and a half, I finished my industrial prototype. As I mentioned before, the unit should have been 16 feet long in order to be as productive as possible (length of panels normally). I was running out of space, and wanted to save money, so I made a smaller one.

The water control unit, where there are the ph and ppm sensors placed above it. The three small peristaltic pumps work when there is an imbalance of ph or ppm. there is a aquarium pump inside the basin to stir the water when a peristaltic pump sends its additive.

 The motor and the sliding system. In the small black box it's the temperature and humidity sensor.

The control computer where it is possible to change parameters, such as motor speed, ppm level, ph level. It is also possible to stop and restart the stepper motor and the misters. The program also allows real-time monitoring of pressure ph, ppm, temperature and humidity data.
