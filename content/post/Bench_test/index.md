# October 20, 2022

Cyclic compression testing platform

The mechanical structure consists of a rigid metal frame supporting a moving platform driven by two stepper motors, positioned on either side of the bench to ensure symmetrical and controlled movement. Each side of the platform is equipped with a force sensor, allowing independent measurement of the forces applied during compression tests.

The motors are controlled by two controllers connected to an Arduino microcontroller. This microcontroller manages the force sensors and handles the acquisition and compilation of measurement data. Before use, the sensors were calibrated using reference weights weighed on a calibrated balance, guaranteeing the accuracy of the force measurements.

A Raspberry Pi computer runs a Python script incorporating a user interface developed with the Tkinter library. This interface allows control of the various functions of the test bench using control buttons, including:

raising or lowering the platform,

executing compression cycles,

independent control of the left or right side.

The system is equipped with a touchscreen, allowing direct use of the test bench without external peripherals. The entire system forms a self-contained platform enabling repetitive testing and the collection of force data for analyzing the compressive behavior of the tested samples.

Technical Description – Cleaning Agent Resistance Test Bench

The developed system is an automated test bench designed to evaluate the resistance of labels subjected to friction cycles and repeated exposure to various cleaning products.

The bench consists of a linear mechanism driven by a mechanical cylinder that performs back-and-forth movements on the surface of the label being tested. This movement simulates a friction or abrasion cycle representative of real-world usage conditions.

A peristaltic pump is integrated into the system to apply the cleaning product to the sample in a controlled manner. After a predefined number of cycles—typically every 20 repetitions—the system automatically sprays a dose of product when the cylinder reaches its minimum position, thus ensuring consistent and reproducible application of the liquid to the label.

The entire system is controlled by an Arduino microcontroller, which manages the sequence of mechanical cycles as well as the activation of the peristaltic pump. Test data and operating parameters are recorded and processed using a Python script, enabling test monitoring and results analysis.

This test bench allows for the controlled reproduction of wear conditions combining mechanical abrasion and exposure to cleaning agents, in order to compare the durability and resistance of different labels or materials.

Technical Description – Force and Durability Test Bench for Exoskeleton Mechanisms

The developed system is an instrumented test bench designed to measure the thrust force generated by mechanisms intended for integration into an exoskeleton, while also allowing for the evaluation of their lifespan under cyclic operation.

The bench is equipped with two force sensors that independently measure the forces produced by each of the mechanisms under test. This configuration allows for the characterization and comparison of the performance of the two systems in terms of applied force.

The sensors are connected to an Arduino microcontroller, which acquires force data and counts the operating cycles. This monitoring allows for the evaluation of the durability of the mechanisms and motors by determining the number of cycles completed before performance degradation or failure.

The acquired data is transmitted to a computer where a Python script records the measurements and cycle information for further analysis and processing.

This test bench makes it possible to characterize the mechanical performance of assistance mechanisms while evaluating their lifespan, within the framework of the development and optimization of exoskeleton systems.
