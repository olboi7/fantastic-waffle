# October 20, 2023

Technical Description – Dynamic Joint Decompression Device

This project involves the development of a patented mechanical device that reduces compression on a joint while preserving limb mobility.

The system's development required several design iterations and the exploration of different mechanisms before arriving at the current architecture. These prototyping and testing phases identified the limitations of the initial approaches and guided the design toward a more robust and functional solution. The integration of technology based on flexible fiberglass rods was a key advancement in the project and made the device viable.

The system uses fiberglass rods arranged in a matrix to increase their flexibility and adaptability to movement. These rods are attached to one end of the device and inserted into a mechanical spring that acts as a force-generating element.

A motor or activation mechanism applies force to the spring to compress it. This compression generates an opposing force, producing a decompression effect at the targeted joint.

At the other end, the fiberglass rods remain free to slide, allowing the system to adapt to changes in limb position during movement. The spring thus exerts a constant decompression force, while the flexible rods maintain the mechanism's alignment while allowing joint movement.

This design combines mechanical decompression assistance with a wide range of motion. The developed technology enabled the project to be realized and is now being used by a company in the development of joint assistance solutions.

The images and videos shown below illustrate different iterations of prototypes developed during the design process. These versions allowed for the validation of mechanical principles, the testing of various configurations, and the optimization of the system's behavior. They represent versions prior to the final design and contributed to the completion of the first functional version of the device.
